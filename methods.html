Technical Documentation & Methodology

This application couples high-resolution land cover data matrices with localized hydrologic soils data to generate stormwater runoff yields and annual pollutant load export estimates.

1. Stormwater Runoff Yield Estimation

Runoff depth computations are executed via a modified variant of the NRCS TR-55 Curve Number (CN) methodology. To better capture the structural capture performance of tree canopies, the core water yield framework integrates an explicitly defined canopy interception term described by Hynicka & Divers (2016):

R = (P - Ci - Ia)2 / [ (P - Ci - Ia) + S ]

Where variables are defined explicitly as:

  • R: Computed Runoff Yield Depth (inches)
  • P: Gross 24-Hour Precipitation Depth (inches)
  • Ci: Canopy Interception Parameter Option (inches)
  • Ia: Initial Abstraction (inches), fixed structurally at 0.2 × S
  • S: Potential Maximum Post-Abstraction Soil Retention Capacity (inches), calculated as: S = (1000 / CNfinal) - 10

2. Canopy Interception & CN Modifications

The tree canopy modifier introduces two programmatic mechanics to scale retention capacity up to model the ameliorating impacts of leaf litter, localized roots, and organic soil building:

  1. Direct Interception (Ci): Subtracts a fixed volume threshold directly from the gross incident rainfall sheet before processing baseline infiltration calculations.
  2. Infiltration Adjustment (CNadj): Deducts integer steps directly from the baseline Land Cover Curve Number matrix to simulate increased infiltration capacity.

3. Annual Land-Use Loading Export Profiles

Annual total edge-of-stream nonpoint source pollutant exports are generated dynamically utilizing export matrices sourced from the Chesapeake Assessment Scenario Tool (CAST) Phase 6 Land-Use Model.
